Scoring

We've decided for our first CTF to use Dynamic Scoring, which means that challenges decrease in value as they receive solves. In other words, the more solves a challenge has, the lower its value is to everyone who has solved it.
Each challenge starts with 1000 points, will stop decreasing at 100 points, and it takes 150 solves to reach the minimum value. Since we don't know yet how many people will be participating in our CTF, we might change those values at any time in order to optimize the scoring.
All the scores will be uploaded to CTFTime (link to the event).

Rules and Guidelines:

  1. Feel free to use any publicly available tool to solve the challenges, BUT be sure that the tool will not cause harm to our infrastructure, or to other team.
    If you're not sure if something is a valid method, let us know by contacting us in the methods mentioned above.
  2. We encorage you to work in teams, but note that it is against the rules to use the knowledge of another user/team to solve a challenge. However, you're welcome to use forums (like StackOverflow) and other publicly-available data to learn how to solve a challenge (learning is the point of a CTF at the end).
  3. You must have an account with valid email linked to it to solve our challenges. Each player may only have one account.
  4. As we said - No flag/hint/intel sharing is allowed. Don't accept hints or guidance from anyone except Hexion staff.
  5. Do not attempt to attack or interfere with other teams or any servers used in this competition that are not explicitly designated for being hacked in a problem. Doing so will force us to take action and you'll be disqualified from the competition.
  6. Brute-force is not allowed - unless said otherwise. As we said earlier, if you're not sure - feel free to ask.
  7. Web challenges - Do not use automated tools (like DirBuster).
  8. Don't forget to have fun, the rules are meant for keeping the CTF up and running for your joy (:

Flag Format
